Bio

I am a Ph.D. student at the Max Planck ETH Center for Learning Systems (CLS), advised by Prof. Florian Dörfler and Prof. Michael Muehlebach. I received my master’s degree in Information Science and Technology from the University of Tokyo, Japan, in 2024. During my master's program, I was advised by Prof. Masayuki Fujita at the System Control Laboratory. Also, I was an exchange student at ETH Zürich from 2022 until 2023 and did student projects advised by Prof. Melanie Zeilinger and Prof. John Lygeros. I received my bachelor's degree in Mathematical Engineering and Information Physics from the University of Tokyo in 2022, where I wrote my thesis advised by Prof. Koji Tsumura.

Research Interests

The lack of rigorous theoretical guarantees stands as a fundamental challenge to the widespread deployment of learning-based control algorithms—or machine learning algorithms in general—in real-world systems, especially when safety is critical. To make these powerful tools more available and reliable, my research interests are in the methodological and theoretical advancements in learning-based control algorithms and machine learning algorithms. Through my research, I hope to combine ideas from both machine learning and control theory, in order to explain and enhance the properties of these algorithms, including performance, adaptability, and efficiency.
